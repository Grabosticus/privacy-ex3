For the exercise we only consider black-box access to the model.
Therefore we cannot reproduce everything they did in the paper, such as Accuracy Extraction,
which relies on direct access to the discriminator.

We are reproducing the following things:

1. Black-box fidelity extraction.
For this we sample latent vectors and query our model to create our own "training set".
We then train a new model on this dataset (model stealing). After training we compute two metrics:
a) Fidelity: What is the FID between our stolen model and the real model?
b) Accuracy: What is the FID between our stolen model and the real dataset?

2. Defenses
For this we pretend that the "StyleGAN API, that we query from" uses some output perturbation
mechanisms to modify the images, while still being visually acceptable. Then we train another
model on this and calculate the same metrics as before. For the output perturbation
we add noise from a normal distribution with std=3. This makes the image still look visually acceptable 
(actually nearly indistinguishable from the normal images).
I also added another defense and trained a 4th model. This time  I only sampled 3000 images from the real model and also added output perturbation, to simulate realistic API limits.


The original authors sampled a different number of images and trained different models on them.
Due to resource constraints we will only generate one fake (not-perturbed) dataset of 50,000
images and one fake perturbed dataset of 50,000 images.


Extension of the original paper:
The authors used older models such as PGGAN and trained them on common GAN datasets such as 
CelebA. We will "extend" the paper by using the much newer, StyleGAN2-ADA architecture
and working on a much more difficult dataset: My dataset of impressionist images (https://github.com/Grabosticus/12222190_impressionist-stylegan)
My dataset is made up of different clusters of visually/semantically similar images, found via clustering
of CLIP embeddings generated on the whole dataset.
We train our model on cluster 1 of this. Cluster 1 contains 5,643 portraits. 
Since the distribution of these portraits is way more complex than the distribution of e.g. CelebA,
GANs struggle on this dataset way more. Therefore we also ask the question, how efficient model
stealing is possible when the stolen model is trained on generations from a complex dataset.

What is FID?
FID (Frechet-Inception-Distance) measures the distance between two distributions (of images).
Therefore, smaller FID values indicate a better model.
